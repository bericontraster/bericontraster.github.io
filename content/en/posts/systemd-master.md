# What is Systemd?
Systemd is the default init system and service manager used in most modern Linux distributions. It is responsible for booting the system, starting and managing services (like nginx, ssh, cron), handling dependencies, and maintaining system state. Designed to replace older init systems like SysVinit, systemd provides faster boot times, consistent service control (systemctl), and robust logging through the journalctl utility.
